## TL;DR

Jack Dorsey outlines a new organizational paradigm for Block, moving away from traditional management hierarchies to an "intelligence-based" structure powered by AI. He argues that AI can replace the information-routing function of middle management by maintaining a continuously updated "world model" of the company's operations and a "customer world model" based on transaction data. In this system, the focus shifts to building core financial capabilities, a comprehensive AI world model, an intelligence layer that composes these capabilities into proactive solutions, and user interfaces. This shift empowers individual contributors, cross-functional problem owners (DRIs), and player-coaches at the "edge"—where human intuition and ethical judgment are needed—while eliminating permanent middle management.

## Key Claims & Analysis

### 1. Traditional hierarchy limits information flow and speed

**Claim:** Companies move fast or slow based on information flow, and traditional hierarchy and middle management impede this flow.

**Evidence Provided:** Mentions the Roman military and Marine Corps struggling with the trade-off between span of control and information speed over two thousand years.

**Verification:** Opinion / Historical extrapolation — The concept that hierarchy slows information is a well-known organizational theory principle, though the post states it as a definitive constraint.

**Assessment:** A foundational premise for the post's argument, relying on historical parallels rather than specific data from Block.

### 2. AI can replace the coordination functions of hierarchy

**Claim:** A system can maintain a continuously updated model of an entire business and use it to coordinate work, replacing the need for humans to relay information through layers of management.

**Evidence Provided:** Describes Block's "remote-first" approach where all decisions, code, designs, and plans are machine-readable artifacts that an AI can use to build a "company world model."

**Verification:** Opinion / Future projection — The post presents this as an intention ("we intend to replace") and early-stage transition ("Block is in the early stages"), rather than a fully realized, proven outcome.

**Assessment:** Highly ambitious claim that represents the core thesis. It shifts AI from a "copilot" for individuals to a structural replacement for management.

### 3. Financial transaction data provides the necessary "honest signal" for AI

**Claim:** Money is the most honest signal, and Block's massive volume of transaction data provides the rich customer signal required to make the "customer world model" useful.

**Evidence Provided:** Contrasts financial transactions ("when they spend, save, send, borrow") with less reliable data sources like surveys and ad clicks.

**Verification:** Verified (Logically) — It's a sound business argument that transaction data reflects actual behavior better than stated intent.

**Assessment:** A strong point that leverages Block's specific advantage in the fintech space.

### 4. The new organizational structure consists of three core roles

**Claim:** The organization normalizes down to Individual Contributors (ICs), Directly Responsible Individuals (DRIs), and Player-coaches, eliminating permanent middle management.

**Evidence Provided:** Defines the specific responsibilities of each role within the new AI-driven context (e.g., player-coaches handle craft and people, not status meetings or alignment, because the "world model handles alignment").

**Verification:** Opinion / Organizational design — This is Block's proposed operational model.

**Assessment:** A radical departure from standard corporate structures, heavily reliant on the assumption that the AI "world model" can truly handle cross-team alignment.

## Practical Takeaways

- **Rethink AI's Role:** Instead of just using AI as a productivity enhancer (a "copilot") within existing structures, consider how it could fundamental alter or replace the structure itself by handling information routing and alignment.
- **Build "World Models":** Organizations with highly digitized, machine-readable artifacts (code, decisions, plans) are best positioned to leverage AI to create real-time, comprehensive views of their operations.
- **Value "Honest Signals":** The effectiveness of organizational AI depends heavily on the quality and honesty of the underlying data (e.g., transaction data vs. survey data).
- **Empower the "Edge":** If AI handles coordination and alignment, humans should be pushed to the "edge" to focus on areas AI cannot reach: intuition, cultural context, ethical decisions, and high-stakes judgment calls.
- **Re-evaluate Management:** Transition the role of managers from information routers and alignment facilitators to "player-coaches" focused on craft, building, and developing people.

## Critical Evaluation (Devil's Advocate)

### Strengths
- Offers a bold, distinct vision for AI integration that goes far beyond standard "efficiency" narratives.
- Logically connects a company's data advantage (transaction volume) to a structural advantage.
- Provides a clear taxonomy of what to build (capabilities, world model, intelligence layer, interfaces) and the roles needed to build it (ICs, DRIs, Player-coaches).

### Weaknesses & Missing Context
- **Practical feasibility:** The post glosses over the immense technical and cultural difficulty of building an AI "world model" that is accurate and trusted enough to actually replace human alignment and prioritization. "Parts of it will likely break before they work" is a massive understatement.
- **Human dynamics:** It assumes that "alignment" is purely an information problem that a model can solve. It ignores the political, emotional, and persuasive aspects of alignment that middle managers often handle. How does the model resolve fundamental disagreements between DRIs?
- **Data privacy and ethics:** A "per-customer, per-merchant representation built from proprietary transaction data" combined with a highly automated "intelligence layer" raises significant privacy and algorithmic bias concerns that are entirely unaddressed.
- **Existential Risk:** Pushing all humans to the "edge" while the core system makes composable decisions autonomously carries significant risk if the system misunderstands context or misaligns capabilities.

### Commercial Context
The post is inherently promotional. It positions Block as a visionary, cutting-edge company leading a historical shift in organizational design. This narrative helps attract top-tier engineering talent interested in novel AI applications and signals to investors that Block is fundamentally restructuring for massive scale and efficiency, rather than just incrementally adopting tools.
